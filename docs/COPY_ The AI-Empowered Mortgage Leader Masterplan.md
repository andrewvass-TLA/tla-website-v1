## You Are Managing Two Types of Originators: The 'Has-Been's and The 'Will-Be's

As a leader of mortgage originators, you've probably noticed something quietly unsettling on your team.

The originators who closed $100 million in 2020 or 2021 are now closing a fraction of that. Some months, maybe nothing at all.

They’re still on the team, still talking about how great things used to be…*and still costing the company every month.* 

Credit reports. Compliance. IT. Technology stacks. Equipment. Support staff. The cost to house them has gone up — but their production has crashed.

While that's happening, other originators on the team (many of them newer, with five to seven years in the industry) are absolutely taking off.

Same market. Same rates. Same headwinds. Wildly different outcomes. This is the dichotomy every mortgage leader is grappling with right now, and the gap between them is getting wider every single month.

The real challenge we all face as leaders is how to inspire both our up-and-comers AND those who have been around for a long time but lost the fire that once drove them to be a top performer.

---

## What's Actually Causing The Gap

It's easy to chalk this up to age, hustle, or attitude. But that's not what's actually happening.

The originators who are skyrocketing in this market aren't necessarily working harder than the ones who are stuck. They're operating differently. They're taking full advantage of AI, and they are inspired about what they are building and the community they surround themselves with.

Tasks that used to take 30 or 60 minutes now take 10 or 15\. Decisions that used to require a manager's input get made in real time, with better reasoning behind them. Conversations that used to vary wildly in quality now consistently convert at a higher level.

When you remove the friction that is commonplace with mortgage work, inspiration and excitement reemerge.

**Every LO Knows Their Numbers — And So Do You**

Most LOs are working inside their pipeline without being able to clearly see it. They're busy. They're active. But neither they nor their managers have real clarity on the critical drivers of the business.

This isn't just an LO problem — it's an executive problem. You can't forecast, coach, or scale what you can't see. And you certainly can't have an honest conversation with a struggling producer about what's actually happening in their business if neither of you has the data.

AI-empowered LOs use AI to gather and analyze that data on their own pipeline. This gives leadership a measurable system at every level built on key metrics:

* Referral performance  
* Lead source quality  
* Conversion rates  
* Revenue per client  
* Time allocation  
* Hidden growth levers

When every LO on your team knows their numbers, your branch stops guessing. Coaching conversations get sharper. Forecasting gets more accurate.

Multiply that across an entire branch and you get the inconsistent month-over-month production most leaders are battling right now.

This is also where the gap between the 'will-be's and the 'has-been's shows up most clearly. The 'will-be's aren't smarter. They're just executing on the right things, every week, without it requiring heroic willpower.

* Turn goals into daily action  
* Reduce distraction and decision fatigue

When your team operates with AI-powered business plans, execution becomes consistent. Consistency drives predictable production. Your forecast starts to mean something.

## AI-Powered Performance Coach

**Targeted Development for Every LO — Without Burning Out Your Managers**

Most LOs lack direction. They consume content, ideas, and training — but don't always know what to focus on next. And realistically, your branch managers don't have time to sit beside every LO and answer that question one-on-one, every week.

This is one of the quietest reasons struggling producers stay struggling. Nobody has the bandwidth to tell them precisely what skill to work on next, in the right order, at the right depth. So they drift. The 'will-be's figure it out for themselves with AI. The 'has-been's don't.

Growth comes from learning the right skill at the right time. AI-empowered LOs use AI agents as their personal performance coaches. They use specially trained AI coaches to:

* Identify their current growth bottleneck  
* Determine the most important next skill  
* Find the most relevant training  
* Connect learning to implementation  
* Improve faster through development

Instead of random learning, growth becomes directed — and your management team gets to focus on real coaching instead of basic skill triage. Your team improves faster, ramps faster, and works on the skills that matter most right now.
